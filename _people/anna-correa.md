Anna Corrêa is a Data Scientist based in Brazil interested in the intersection of technology and art: specifically in what AI-generated poetry reveals about the human conventions embedded in literary canons.

<!--more-->

Her fellowship research compares canonical human poetry from the Poetry Foundation corpus to AI-generated poems through a blended methodology of computational analysis and close reading, asking whose voices and forms are reproduced (and whose are erased) when a machine imitates a poet.

She studied in the United States where she engaged in community-led initiatives through Phi Theta Kappa, winning two international awards, and within her community college. She also received the Nelson Sepulveda Leadership Award for her work at a collegewide conference on AI and its fair use. Anna writes on the side and has been published in literary journals.

Anna will begin her Masters at the London Interdisciplinary School in September 2026, where she will continue researching the boundaries between human and machine expression. She writes on [Medium](https://medium.com/@annakarolynepc) and can be found on [GitHub](https://github.com/annapcorrea).
